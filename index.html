Sociology

Sociology is the study of social life, social change, and the social causes and consequences of human behavior.

Family

Family constitutes two or more people related by blood, marriage or adoption living together.

Family, as a basic unit of society, is the building block upon which a society is constructed.

Types of Families:-
  • Joint Family
  • Extended Family
  • Nuclear Family
  • Single Parent Family
Joint Family

A family where grandparents, parents, siblings and cousins live together.

Extended Family

A family where parents, grandparents (mostly paternal) and ocasionally, unmarried aunts and uncles live in the same household.

Nuclear Family

A family where husband and wife live with their children.

Single Parent Family

A family where one parent, often the mother, resides along with her childrens.

Family Structure & Functioning

A way in which the family is organised in terms of it's boundaries , emotional bonding and interactions outside the family.

  • A family that has closed boundries allowing minimal or no interaction with other units of the society, leads to enmeshment or loss of boundaries within the family. This results in mental and physical health problems.
  • A family with open boundaries, with strong ties to each other forms the basis of healthy interactions as well as better physical and emotional health in it's members.

An ill member of the family changes the structure and functioning of the basic unit.

The influence is maximally scene in psychiatric disorders, chronic illnesses, head injuries, stroke and cancer.

In our social settings, the entire burden of care falls on one member of the family.

A sensitive health professional is familier with the concept of the carer's burden and it's impact on the health of the carer.

A health professional aware of these basic concepts can easily win over the key member of the family.

They will then include him/her in the decision making process to ensure their positive input.

Social Class

Define:-

All societies have ways of placing people in social strata. These can be based on wealth, education, inheritance or other criteria.

The western method of dividing society into six classes as follows:

  • Professional
  • Managers & Technicians
  • Non-Manual Skilled
  • Manual Skilled
  • Partly Skilled
  • Unskilled

In our society individuals are classified on account of their land holding and professions into:

  • Feudal
  • Businessman
  • Technician
  • Farmer
  • Labourer

Health parameters may greatly vary amongst classes.

  1. Lower socioeconomic classes really largerly on public sector health facilities or are at the mercy of non professional health workers such as quacks, amils & charlatans
  2. The higher social classes largely rely on private healthcare infrastructure or treatment in other countries.

From a B.S perspective it is useful to divide these sections of the society into the advantaged and the disadvantaged. This division is based on access to education, scientific & modern health services, clean water, senitation & shelter.

Impact of Social Factor on Treatment Adherence (Compliance)

Failure to follow health related advise is widespread. According to the WHO upto 50% patients donot take medications as advised.

This includes taking medication incorrectly or not at all, forgeting or refusing to make essential behavioural changes and presisting in behaviours such as smoking that may jeopardise their health.

Failure to adhere to treatment is determined by:

  • Patient's age
  • Socioeconomic status
  • Education status
  • Type & chronicity of illness
  • Lack of social support
  • Patient-doctor trust
  • Faliure to understand the doctore's advise
  • Depression
  • Cost of drug
  • Dosing frequency

Some of the clues which should alert a physician that a patient might not be adhering to the treatment plan are as follows:

  1. Lack of involvement in the treatment
  2. Depressed Patient
  3. Lack of response
  4. Confusing Clinical Picture

Essential elements required to improve a patient's compliance are summarised below:

  1. Accurate Communication of information between patient and doctor
  2. Emotional support for the patient
  3. Recognition of the patient's depression or hopelessness
  4. Focus on the overall quality of life of the patient

Anthropology

Anthropology is the study of evolution of civilizations, their social characteristics, languages,cultures and way of life.

In a culturally sensitive health care approach social aspects are treated at par with biological determinants of disease. the social dimension of health is thus an integral part of history taking, assessment diagnostics and therapeutics

Culture

Culture is the set of values, mores, beliefs & perceptions of the world that are passed on from one generation to the next.

we share it with other members of our society as our common world view.

Encourages group survival and gives it's members a useful picture of the universe.

Subcultures

A subculture is a group within a culture that differs from the general consensus. They have a unique set of beliefs and values that don't necessarily align with the wider culture.

Understanding Culture in Health Care

Beliefs, Values, Norms, Mores, Forkways & Law from the fabric of society. They are the basic for social control and do's and don't of cultures and subcultures

Belief

A belief is an attitude that something is the case, or that some proposition about the world is true.

Values

Values are those aspects of a culture that are held in high regard are desirable and therefore worthy of emulation.

Norms

Norms referred to principles of right and wrong that government acceptable and unacceptable behaviour in a society.

Folkways

Folkways are customary patterns of everyday life that specify what is socially correct.

Mores

Mores are considered vital for the groups welfare and survival thus making them very important.

Laws

Laws are formally complied written rules and regulations coded after debate and deliberation.
